Ronday Technologies

Summary: For my first design job, I worked at the tech startup Ronday Technologies on their online 3D conferencing and hangout platform. As to be expected of startup work, I performed a myriad of tasks both design-related and otherwise.

Skills: UX Design, UX Copy, User Research, Competitive Analysis, Graphic Design, Video Directing, Video Recording, & Video Editing

Time spent: 7 months.

Working at Ronday was a blast. I found it delightful to work on an innovative project with such a passionate team. I also had ample opportunities for self-growth, as I had to teach myself graphic design, video editing, and more.

The results speak for themselves. My wallboards are the default images on all of Ronday's most popular maps. My Twitter Ads boast a conversion rate 20x higher than the industry average. My research results have guided major decisions for the company.

Here is a full list of my accomplishments at Ronday:

  • I designed 130 wallboards (75 final versions), 3 user flows, and 4 high fidelity wireframes
  • I created the majority (90%+) of the Ronday Help Center.
  • I wrote a user research plan. Then using that plan, I participated in 3 user interviews, and led 3 more.
  • I performed competitive research on 13 competitors and presented my findings to the company.
  • I also directed, recorded, and edited 5 Twitter advertisements and a Discord bot advertisement.
  • Participated in countless design discussions to improve the Ronday platform.

I was tasked with creating the help center. At the time, there were only a few pages:

  • Keyboard shortcuts
  • Customizing wallboards
  • Managing the Ronday Group
  • Build notes, and a link to the Ronday platform

So, I worked alongside Senior Design employee Chris Tung to create an information hierarchy which outlined everything which needed including.

Except for the Links category, each category had 3-4 individual pages. Every page had 3-7 sections of written and visual content. For each page, I did the following process:

  1. Write the script containing all necessary information
  2. Verify that the writing was accessible (see photo)
  3. Record and edit 10-30 second videos of me performing the task
  4. Receive final feedback from co-workers

I took great care to ensure that the articles were as succinct and accessible as possible. For example, the original visual content were GIFs, but I know that autoplaying content which cannot be paused is not accessible (See WCAG 2.2).

Through user testing, we determined what content was missing and what needed improvement. So the development was an ongoing process which continues to this day.

In total I completed 10 help center articles with 32 sections in total. Of the edited visual content, there are 14 videos, and 16 images.
