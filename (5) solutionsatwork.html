Solutions at Work

Summary: I work at the Cambridge, Massachusetts nonprofit Solutions at Work. The organization suffered from a terribly dated website and social media. As such, I single handedly modernized their entire online presence by overhauling the website, creating infographics, a design system, conducting user research, and more.

Skills: UX Design, UX Copy, User Research, Graphic Design, Technical Administration

Time spent: >1 year.

I completely overhauled the online presence of Solutions at Work. It began with an assessment of the website. The interface was cluttered, and was nearly unusable on mobile. Furthermore, the information was months out of date with no consistency in the information hierarchy. So, I created a design system to overhaul the entire website’s content and design. Since the redesign, I conduct user research on the website using Hotjar.

Additionally, I volunteer as a technical administrator. I manage organization emails, domain certificates, writing and sending newsletters to over 1300 people, and managing our contacts database. Furthermore, I serve on the media committee where we make decisions for upcoming events and donation drives.

My design process began by reviewing some well known design systems - namely M3 and Shopify. The website is meant primarily to convince users to donate to the cause, so I knew the system need not be very in-depth. Then, I did some brief reviews of the 100+ organizations we have partnered with in the past. From there I made the following design decisions:

  • Our primary target users are people who can donate, not people in need of donations
  • For ethical reasons, we will use positive language to celebrate making a difference over guilting users about how difficult it is to be homeless
  • In addition to positive language, we will use bright and warm colors
  • Our website containers will have rounded edges

After initial construction of the design system, I went over it once again with an eye for accessibility

  1. All images must have alt text
  2. As many users are elderly, text contrast must be at least 7:1
  3. Reading level of content must be kept beneath secondary school reading level
  4. Removal of time-sensitive content
  5. Layout must work on desktop, tablet, and mobile

Design systems pose serious advantages - creating designs are much quicker, fewer revisions are needed, and they allow entire organisations to maintain consistency. This consistency persists no matter the size of the organisation or if assignments change hands.

However, design systems require a lot of effort to update and maintain. One small change could cause ripples which take several hours to apply. In other words, design systems promote efficiency within consistency, but require extra effort to remain flexible.

If you are wondering, yes it is nearing ridiculous to make an entire design system for an organization of this small of a size. However, it was an opportunity for me to learn and so I seized it with both arms! While the scope of the designs I make for this organization are limited, by studying Shopify and M3's design systems in depth, I have definitively grown as a designer.

As the only designer on the team, I’ve been fortunate to have the control enough to design and run my own user tests. By analyzing heatmaps and viewing user recordings, I have been able to make demonstrable improvements to user experience.

Improving information hierarchy

  • Problem: Our contact email kept receiving questions that should’ve been easily answered by the website
  • Research: Analyzing 10 random clickmaps from people who visited donations
  • Findings: The information hierarchy was poor which discouraged users from scrolling far enough
  • Result: After changing it, the number of emails about our hours/locations dropped by 60%

Distinguishing Headers from Buttons

  • Problem: As shown in the heat map, several users thought the header was a button
  • Research: A/B Test on a new style of header
  • Result: Fewer clicks on non-interactable elements

Reducing visual clutter on mobile

  • Problem: By reviewing user recordings, I noticed a large number of mobile users were scrolling all the way down, and all the way up on the donation page looking for information
  • Research: A/B Test on a new mobile interface
  • Result: Designed a more streamlined donation page which reduced the page's bounce rate by 11%

I have never met another group so passionate and hardworking just because they know it to be right. That alone has made the entire experience worthwhile. There were a few pain points however, as are all working around other jobs and responsibilities. A lack of resources is the most common. But, I have thoroughly enjoyed learning and improving beyond my skills as a UX Designer.
